  1. NeurIPS ’26
    Deep Research as Rubric for Reinforcement Learning
    Wangyi Mei and others
    In Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS), 2026
    Preprint, 2025. Under review at NeurIPS 2026.
    Abs arXiv Bib Code

    Open-ended reasoning and long-form generation lack reference answers, making verifiable rewards hard to construct. DR-Rubric splits reward design into a two-stage pipeline — task-relevant fact collection followed by fine-grained rubric generation — auto-generating per-query evaluation criteria for GRPO training. With only 1K–3K rubric-based RL instances, an 8B model matches or exceeds baselines trained on 16K–90K instances. A self-bootstrapping scheme lets the policy itself produce next-round rubrics, and we diagnose the non-monotonic bootstrap trajectory with a reward polarization metric.

  2. EMNLP ’26
    Learning To Think In Blocks: A Prior-Guided Reinforcement Learning Framework for Deep Research
    Wangyi Mei and others
    In Conference on Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing (EMNLP), 2026
    Preprint, 2025. Under review at EMNLP 2026.
    Abs Bib

    BGPO models each (think, toolcall) assistant turn as a minimal semantic decision block: length normalization within a block, equal-weight geometric aggregation across blocks. Credit is assigned by research decision, not token length. Training-side ratio variance drops  17× over GRPO/DAPO, clipping effectively vanishes. A prior-guided warm start (decomposition, keyword extraction, HyDE, etc.) seeds the action space; BGPO then learns action composition from environment feedback. On 7 QA tasks 7B Qwen2.5+BGPO averages 0.708 vs 0.664 for Search-R1-7B; 14B reaches 0.767.

  1. ACL ’25
    Logical Consistency is Vital: Neural-Symbolic Information Retrieval for Negative-Constraint Queries
    Guanghui* Xu, Zhe* Zhang, Wangyi* Mei, and 1 more author
    In Proceedings of the 63rd Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics (ACL), 2025
    *Equal contribution. Accepted to ACL 2025 (CCF-A).
    Abs Bib

    Dense retrievers over-rely on lexical co-occurrence and struggle with negative constraints ("exclude"/"without" conditions). We extend retrieval from pure semantic similarity to "semantically relevant + logically consistent" re-ranking. NS-IR converts query/document to first-order logic, aligns logic semantics, and applies negative-constraint-aware token reweighting. On the new NegConstraint benchmark it lifts MAP/nDCG@10 from BGE’s 36.3/40.8 to 53.3/56.5, surpassing HyDE and InteR.
